Welcome to my nightmare.

Reader, I am angry. Food media is a stale, terrible state of the boring. Lifestyle blogs are hardly more than "look at the lightroom edits I made", and may likely be the single biggest consumers to demand for 50mm and macro lenses.

It has become an industry of simplicity, of trends, of ignorance of sustainability, of nothing more than #foodporn, HDR and oversaturation of the same lighting and stylings as fast as they can be churned out, with no regard to the substance of your recipe. In fact, I challenge you to find a recipe from a lifestyle blog that offers a unique take a particular dish, with more than "adapted from (x)" as it's modifier. Best of luck to you.

I am not here for that. I am here to rant, rave, and be disgusted. I am here to display my increased blood pressure in the form of 900 or so words, but I am also here to help you. I want us to get back to basics. I want to get away from cheese covered plates and bottled dressings.

I intend to use this site, not to drag content creators--but to be continually appalled at food trends, and to offer better, more realistic versions of popular recipes. Your pictures of muffin tin frittatas look amazing, Kelsie. But if I can't replicate that result at home, what's the point?

So let's do this. Let's buck the trend. Let's make food better, instead of just taking better pictures.

Join me in saying: Fuck Your Food Blog.

And let's make food better.
